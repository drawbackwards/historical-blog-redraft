# Design Trends 2024

Design never stands still — and 2024 is proving to be one of its most dynamic years yet. From AI-assisted workflows reshaping how we create, to a cultural backlash against sterile minimalism, the forces driving visual culture right now are anything but predictable. Here's our take on the trends defining the year, and what they mean for brands willing to pay attention.

## AI-Generated Visuals

Artificial intelligence has moved from novelty to fixture in the designer's toolkit. Tools like Midjourney, DALL-E, and Adobe Firefly have fundamentally changed the pace at which ideas can be visualized — compressing what once took hours of concepting and asset production into minutes.

The conversation around AI in design is still charged, and understandably so. Questions of authorship, originality, and creative integrity deserve serious consideration. But we see AI most powerfully as an amplifier of human creativity, not a replacement for it. In the right hands, these tools accelerate the exploratory phases of a project — rapidly generating visual directions, testing tonal ranges, and pressure-testing concepts — freeing designers to spend more time on what truly requires craft and judgment. The key is intentionality. AI-generated visuals used uncritically produce work that feels hollow and generic; used thoughtfully, they can unlock creative territory that would otherwise be out of reach.

## Brutalism Is Back

After years of rounded corners, generous whitespace, and the quiet confidence of Swiss-inspired minimalism, the design world is craving friction again. Brutalism — with its raw typography, clashing color palettes, exposed grid structures, and deliberately asymmetric layouts — has made a forceful return, particularly in web and editorial design.

This isn't mere nostalgia. The resurgence of brutalist aesthetics reflects a broader cultural appetite for authenticity and edge in an era of brand homogeneity. When every startup seems to share the same sans-serif wordmark and pastel palette, brutalism feels genuinely rebellious — and for brands targeting audiences who can spot and reject insincerity from a mile away, that's a powerful differentiator. Done well, brutalism isn't ugly; it's honest. It challenges the viewer, commands attention, and makes a clear statement that the brand behind it isn't trying to please everyone.

## 3D Everything

Dimensional design has arrived in full force, and it's no longer the exclusive territory of big-budget campaigns with dedicated 3D studios. Advances in real-time rendering, WebGL, and accessible tools like Spline and Blender have lowered the barrier to entry significantly, making immersive 3D elements a viable option for a much wider range of projects.

We're seeing 3D deployed across product pages, hero sections, brand identity systems, and app interfaces — often blended with flat design in ways that create striking visual depth without sacrificing load performance. Beyond aesthetics, 3D serves a functional purpose: it lets users interact with products and spaces in ways that feel more intuitive and exploratory. For brands in e-commerce, architecture, or technology, this kind of spatial storytelling isn't just a visual treat — it's a genuinely better way to communicate.

## Sustainability in Design

Environmental responsibility has moved from optional brand messaging to a baseline expectation, and design is both reflecting and driving that shift. Earthy palettes, organic textures, nature-derived imagery, and uncoated-stock aesthetics signal a brand's alignment with ecological values — but the most sophisticated sustainability-driven design goes well beyond surface choices.

Forward-thinking studios and in-house teams are increasingly considering the environmental footprint of the work itself: optimizing digital products for energy efficiency, reducing print waste through smarter material specifications, and building visual systems that are built to last rather than trend-cycle out in 18 months. Sustainability in design isn't a stylistic phase — it's a disciplinary shift. Brands that treat it as a genuine creative and strategic commitment, rather than a palette swap, are the ones building lasting credibility with audiences who increasingly hold them accountable.

## Conclusion

The most exciting thing about 2024's design landscape isn't any single trend — it's the productive tension between them. AI and handcraft. Brutality and beauty. Immersion and restraint. Sustainability and spectacle. The designers and brands navigating these tensions with intention are the ones producing the most compelling, resonant work.
