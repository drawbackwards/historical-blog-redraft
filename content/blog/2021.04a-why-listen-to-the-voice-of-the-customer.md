*By Ward Andrews*

A Voice of the Customer (VOC) program is how businesses systematically listen to what customers are saying, extract meaningful insight from it, and turn that feedback into real business results. Without one, you're guessing.

## Why Do You Need a Voice of the Customer Program?

Are your customers happy? How do you know for sure? And if they're not happy, do you know exactly why and how to fix it?

These are questions that plague businesses of all types, but very few know how to answer them. Even fewer know how to extract meaningful insights from the answers they do get.

Customer experience drives your competitive advantage. It's vital for building both employee and customer loyalty and solidifying your brand. That's why you need to be listening not once in a while, but every day.

Customers demand personalized experiences and they're quite vocal when they don't get what they want. They can either praise or slam you for all the world to see with a few taps of their fingers.

You'd think all the data out there would make customers and businesses tighter than ever. But that data creates a lot of noise that can be hard to put into context. A strong Voice of the Customer program cuts through the noise.

Before you can even think about building a Voice of the Customer program, you need to know your CX maturity.

## What Is Customer Experience (CX) Maturity and Why Does It Matter?

CX maturity is a measure of your organization's commitment to a customer-centric mindset. The more you integrate customer experience into your day-to-day operations, the higher you move up the maturity scale.

It's worth distinguishing from UX maturity here. UX maturity measures the user experience with your specific products or services. CX measures their experience with all of your services, products, employees, and brand as a whole.

Most organizations, due to a lack of time or resources, are stuck somewhere in the middle. They collect a bunch of customer experience data but don't really know what to do with it or how to integrate it into their organization.

## How Does CX Maturity Relate to a VOC Program?

To build an effective Voice of the Customer program, you must increase your CX maturity. At the same time, a strong Voice of the Customer program can improve your CX maturity. It's a symbiotic relationship that pays dividends in business growth.

The more you move up the CX maturity scale, the more customer loyalty and greater financial results you're likely to see. Analysis from the sales and marketing experts at Maritz suggests that companies at the highest levels of their CX maturity model are three times more likely to have better financial performance and customer retention compared to companies in the lower half.

In short, it pays to increase your CX maturity. A best-in-class Voice of the Customer program can transform customer feedback into real business results. But it takes time, money, and especially dedication to get it right.

## FAQ

**What is a Voice of the Customer (VOC) program?**
A Voice of the Customer program is a structured approach to collecting, analyzing, and acting on customer feedback. It helps businesses understand what customers actually think and need, rather than relying on assumptions.

**How is CX maturity different from UX maturity?**
UX maturity measures how well your organization designs experiences for specific products or services. CX maturity is broader. It covers your customers' experience with your entire brand, including every product, service, employee interaction, and touchpoint.

**Why do most companies struggle with customer experience data?**
Most organizations collect customer experience data but don't know how to integrate it into their operations. They're stuck in the middle of the CX maturity scale, generating noise without extracting signal.
