# How Do You Boost User Engagement?

**By Ward Andrews**

The not-so-secret trick to improving user engagement is this: give people what they want, when they want it. That means designing with a constant focus on user needs, anticipating what comes next, and providing the right context at the right moment.

## What Is Causing Low User Engagement?

We get this question from our clients all the time. People aren't logging in to their app enough. They're not using the fancy new features that got built. Users don't seem to know how to engage with the product, or they're not taking advantage of upgrade options.

The problem usually isn't the product itself. It's that product teams have lost their way in a dense fog of competing business priorities and urgent feature requests. When that happens, user needs stop driving decisions -- and engagement drops.

## What Are the Three Principles of User Engagement?

Through a series of blog posts this month, we're exploring the three main principles of user engagement that we use to help clients reach their business and UX goals:

1. **Prioritise Jobs-To-Be-Done** -- Understand the real tasks your users are trying to accomplish, and design around those.
2. **Anticipate User Needs** -- Get ahead of what users need next before they have to ask for it.
3. **Provide the Right Context** -- Deliver the right information, prompt, or feature at the moment it's actually useful.

Each principle builds on the last. Together, they form a foundation for thoughtful design that keeps users coming back.

## How Do You Build a Product That Keeps Users Coming Back?

Thoughtful design that anticipates the needs of your users keeps them coming back for more. But it can't be built in a day.

It takes a constant focus on continuous improvement -- from initial research that uncovers the needs of both users and the business, to creating and iterating on solutions to find what works best. There's no shortcut past that process, but there is a clear path through it.

## FAQ

**What is the most effective way to boost user engagement?**
Give users what they want, when they want it. That means prioritising their Jobs-To-Be-Done, anticipating what they need next, and surfacing the right context at the right moment.

**Why aren't users engaging with new features in my app?**
Usually it comes down to a disconnect between what the product team built and what users actually need. When competing business priorities crowd out user research, features get built that don't map to real user goals -- and engagement suffers.

**What are Jobs-To-Be-Done and why do they matter for UX?**
Jobs-To-Be-Done is a framework for understanding the real task a user is trying to accomplish -- not just what they click, but why they're there in the first place. Designing around those jobs means your product is solving actual problems, which drives engagement.

**How long does it take to improve user engagement through better UX design?**
It's not a one-time fix. Improving user engagement takes continuous iteration -- starting with research that surfaces user and business needs, then designing, testing, and refining solutions over time.

**How is anticipating user needs different from just adding more features?**
Anticipating user needs means knowing what users need before they ask, based on research and context -- not stacking on features for the sake of it. More features can actually hurt engagement if they add noise without solving real problems.
